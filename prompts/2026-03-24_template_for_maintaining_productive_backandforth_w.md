# Productive AI Conversation Templates

After thousands of back-and-forth exchanges with AI, I've learned that most unproductive conversations follow predictable patterns. Here are templates that consistently work.

## The Context-Heavy Opener

**Instead of:** "Help me write marketing copy"
**Try this structure:**
```
Context: [Your business/situation in 2-3 sentences]
Goal: [Specific outcome you want]
Constraints: [What you can't do/requirements]
Starting point: [What you already have]
```

**Real example:** "Context: I run a 15-person consulting firm specializing in supply chain optimization. Goal: Email sequence for prospects who downloaded our warehouse efficiency guide but haven't responded. Constraints: Can't be pushy, need to demonstrate expertise without giving everything away. Starting point: I have their download data and know they're operations managers."

This gets me usable first drafts about 70% of the time, versus maybe 20% with vague requests.

## The Iterative Refinement Loop

Once you get initial output, don't start over. Build on it:

**Template:**
```
"This is close. Three adjustments:
1. [Specific change with example]
2. [Different tone/style request]
3. [Addition or removal]

Keep everything else the same."
```

**What works:** Being surgical about changes. AI handles incremental improvements much better than complete overhauls.

**What doesn't:** Saying "make it better" or "more professional." Be specific.

## The Perspective Shift Prompt

When stuck, change the angle:

```
"Approach this same problem as if you were:
- [Different role/perspective]
- Solving for [different constraint]
- In [different context/timeframe]
```

**Real example:** I was stuck on a project timeline. Asked AI to approach it "as if you were a project manager who consistently delivers early" versus my original "help me plan this project." Got completely different, more actionable advice.

## The Reality Check Template

Use this when AI gets too theoretical:

```
"Ground this in reality. What would actually happen if:
- I only have 2 hours today
- My boss questions the approach
- The first attempt fails

Give me the practical version."
```

## Common Pitfalls I've Learned

**The approval trap:** Asking "Is this good?" AI almost always says yes. Instead: "What's the weakest part of this approach?"

**The perfectionism spiral:** Asking for endless refinements. Set a 3-iteration limit for most tasks.

**The context dump:** Sharing your entire company history. AI works better with focused, relevant context.

## The Honest Assessment Close

End conversations with:
```
"What am I not considering? What could go wrong with this approach?"
```

This catches blind spots about 40% of the time. Worth the extra exchange.

**Bottom line:** Treat AI like a capable colleague who needs clear instructions and benefits from structured feedback. The magic isn't in perfect prompts—it's in productive iteration patterns.
