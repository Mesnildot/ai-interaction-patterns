# Daily AI Workflow Optimization Templates

## The "Context-First" Daily Kickoff

```
I'm starting a [type of work session] focused on [main objective]. 

My constraints today: [time/resources/priorities]
My energy level: [high/moderate/low]
Key deadlines: [what's urgent]

Help me structure the next [timeframe] to maximize both quality and efficiency. 
What should I tackle first, and what can wait?
```

**Why this works:** I've found that front-loading context prevents the AI from giving generic advice. When I mention I'm "low energy with a 2pm deadline," I get fundamentally different suggestions than when I say I'm "high energy with flexible timing."

**Real example:** Yesterday I said I had "moderate energy, 3 hours, need to prep a client presentation." The AI suggested breaking it into 45-minute focused blocks rather than trying to power through. Actually followed that advice and finished 30 minutes early.

## The "Stack and Optimize" Template

```
I regularly do these tasks: [list 3-5 recurring activities]

Current pain points:
- [specific friction you experience]
- [time drains or frustration points]

Show me 2-3 ways to batch, automate, or streamline these. 
Focus on solutions I can implement this week, not major system overhauls.
```

**The honest truth:** This template works about 70% of the time. Sometimes the AI suggests overly complex solutions or assumes you have tools you don't. But when it hits, it really hits. I discovered I could batch similar writing tasks together and use the AI to maintain consistent tone across them.

## The "Decision Triage" Framework

```
I need to decide: [specific decision]

Context: [brief background]
My options: [A, B, C...]
My priorities: [what matters most]

Help me think through this systematically. What am I not considering?
What would you need to know to give better advice?
```

**What doesn't work:** Asking the AI to "just decide for me." It'll give you an answer, but it won't be grounded in your reality.

**What works:** Using it as a thinking partner. I recently used this for choosing between three project approaches. The AI didn't pick for me, but it highlighted risks I'd missed and helped me clarify what I actually cared about.

## The "End-of-Day Reflection"

```
Today I accomplished: [key wins]
What felt inefficient: [specific struggles]
Tomorrow's priority: [one main focus]

Based on today's patterns, suggest 1-2 small adjustments for tomorrow's workflow.
```

**Pattern I've noticed:** The AI is surprisingly good at spotting when you're context-switching too much or taking on low-value tasks. It's less helpful with emotional or interpersonal workflow issues.

## Honest Limitations

These templates assume you can be specific about your work. If you're in exploration mode or dealing with highly sensitive information, they're less useful. Also, the AI tends to be optimistic about how much you can actually change at once. Start with one small tweak, not a complete workflow overhaul.
