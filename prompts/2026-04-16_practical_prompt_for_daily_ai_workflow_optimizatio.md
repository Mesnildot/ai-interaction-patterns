# Daily AI Workflow Optimization Templates

## The Reality Check: What Actually Works

After months of experimenting with AI integration, I've learned that the biggest gains come from **small, consistent optimizations** rather than dramatic overhauls. Here are the prompt patterns that genuinely improve my daily workflow.

## Template 1: The Context Preloader

```
I'm working on [specific project/task]. My role is [your function]. 
The key constraint is [time/quality/resources]. 

Context dump: [2-3 sentences about current situation]

Help me [specific request] by considering:
- What I've already tried: [brief list]
- What I need to avoid: [constraints]
- Success looks like: [concrete outcome]
```

**Why this works:** Front-loading context eliminates 3-4 rounds of clarification. The AI gets your situation immediately instead of guessing.

**Limitation:** Takes 30 seconds more upfront, but saves 5 minutes of back-and-forth.

## Template 2: The Decision Accelerator

```
I need to decide between [Option A] and [Option B] for [specific situation].

My priorities ranked: [1. 2. 3.]
Deadline: [when]
Stakeholders who care: [who and why]

Give me:
- 3 questions I should ask myself
- One potential blindspot I'm missing
- A quick stress-test scenario for each option
```

**Real example:** Used this for choosing between two project management tools. The "blindspot" question revealed I hadn't considered team training time—saved us from a bad choice.

**What doesn't work:** Asking for "the best option." AI can't make decisions for you, but it's excellent at surfacing considerations you missed.

## Template 3: The Daily Optimizer

```
Here's what I need to accomplish today:
[Paste your actual to-do list]

My energy level: [high/medium/low]
Available time blocks: [actual schedule]
Current mood/focus: [honest assessment]

Suggest:
- Which 2 items to tackle first and why
- What to batch together
- One thing I should probably move to tomorrow
```

**Pattern that emerged:** This works best at 9 AM with coffee. By afternoon, I'm too tired to think strategically about task ordering.

**Honest tradeoff:** Sometimes the AI suggests perfectly logical sequences that ignore how I actually work. I've learned to ask for 2-3 options rather than one "optimal" approach.

## What I've Stopped Doing

- **Over-prompting:** Those 500-word prompts with detailed personas? They mostly waste time. AI works better with clear, concise direction.
- **Perfectionist templates:** I used to craft elaborate prompts. Now I iterate fast and adjust based on results.
- **One-size-fits-all:** Different tasks need different prompt styles. Writing needs creative prompts, analysis needs structured ones.

## The Meta-Pattern

The best workflow optimization isn't about perfect prompts—it's about building a feedback loop. Use simple templates, notice what works, adjust quickly. Your workflow is unique, so your prompts should be too.
