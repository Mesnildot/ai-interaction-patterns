# Template for Maintaining Productive Back-and-Forth with AI

The secret to productive AI conversations isn't about crafting the perfect initial prompt—it's about building momentum through structured follow-ups. After thousands of interactions, I've learned that successful AI collaboration is more like jazz improvisation than reading sheet music.

## The Iteration Framework

**Start with scaffolding, not specifics.** Begin conversations by establishing the working relationship:
- "I'm going to work through [problem] with you step by step"
- "I'll share context as we go, so ask questions if something's unclear"
- "Let's iterate on this together"

This primes the AI to expect collaboration rather than one-shot answers.

## Three Follow-Up Patterns That Actually Work

**The Redirect:** When responses drift off-target, don't restart—steer.
- "That's helpful background, but let's focus specifically on..."
- "I see three ideas there. Let's dive deeper into the second one"
- "Good start. Now apply that same logic to..."

**The Build:** When you get something useful, immediately expand it.
- "That structure works. Now fill in examples for each section"
- "Perfect. What would the counterarguments be?"
- "This is exactly what I need. Create three variations with different tones"

**The Reality Check:** When things sound good but feel off, probe deeper.
- "Walk me through how this would work in practice"
- "What am I missing here? What could go wrong?"
- "This seems too smooth. Where are the rough edges?"

## What Breaks Down (And How to Fix It)

**Context decay** happens fast. After 4-5 exchanges, the AI starts forgetting your original goals. Combat this by periodically restating objectives: "Remember, our goal is still to..."

**Scope creep** is real. AI suggestions often expand into adjacent territories. I've found success with explicit boundaries: "Let's solve X before we tackle Y" or "Park that idea—we'll come back to it."

**False momentum** feels like progress but isn't. The AI generates confident-sounding responses that miss the mark. Counter this by asking for specifics: "Give me a concrete example" or "Show me what this looks like with real data."

## The Honest Truth About Limitations

Some conversations just won't work. If you're not getting traction after 6-7 exchanges, the problem is usually:
- Unclear initial framing (restart with better context)
- Mismatched capabilities (AI can't actually do what you're asking)
- Your own unclear thinking (step back and clarify for yourself first)

The best AI collaborations feel like thinking out loud with a very capable colleague who never gets tired, never judges, but also never truly understands stakes or consequences. Lean into that dynamic rather than fighting it.
