# Daily AI Workflow Optimization Templates

I've been experimenting with different approaches to make AI tools actually useful in daily work, not just impressive demos. Here's what I've learned works consistently.

## The "Context Stack" Template

Start every session by building context in layers:

```
Context: I'm a [role] working on [specific project/task]
Goal: [what you want to accomplish today]
Constraints: [time, resources, requirements]
Previous work: [brief summary of where you left off]

Now help me...
```

This beats the scattered "help me write an email" approach. The AI remembers your context and gives more targeted responses throughout the session.

## The "Progressive Refinement" Pattern

Instead of expecting perfect output immediately, use this three-step cycle:

1. **Rough draft**: "Give me a basic outline for..."
2. **Targeted improvement**: "Make section 2 more technical" or "Add examples to the intro"
3. **Final polish**: "Check this for tone and clarity"

I used to get frustrated when first attempts weren't perfect. This approach acknowledges that AI works better as a thinking partner than a magic solution.

## The "Batch Processing" Template

For repetitive tasks, create a format once and reuse it:

```
Format: [define structure]
Tone: [specify style]
Length: [be specific - "2 paragraphs" not "brief"]

Process these [items] using the above format:
[item 1]
[item 2]
[item 3]
```

This works great for social media posts, email responses, or product descriptions. The key is being specific about format upfront.

## What Actually Doesn't Work

**The perfectionist trap**: Spending 20 minutes crafting the "perfect" prompt. Often a quick, conversational prompt works better than an over-engineered one.

**The context dump**: Pasting entire documents and asking "summarize this." Break large tasks into smaller, focused requests.

**The one-and-done mindset**: Expecting to never need follow-up questions. AI works best in conversation, not monologue.

## Daily Integration Tips

I keep a simple note with my most-used prompt templates. Copy-paste saves time and ensures consistency.

Set a "good enough" threshold. AI-generated content that's 80% there and takes 5 minutes to fix often beats starting from scratch.

Use AI for the parts of your work you find tedious, not the parts you enjoy. I use it for first drafts and research, but I still do the creative problem-solving myself.

The biggest workflow improvement? Treating AI as a really smart intern who needs clear direction but can handle the grunt work. Once I stopped expecting it to read my mind, it became genuinely useful.
