# Template for Maintaining Productive Back-and-Forth with AI

The secret to productive AI conversations isn't crafting the perfect first prompt—it's knowing how to maintain momentum through multiple exchanges. After thousands of back-and-forth sessions, I've found a few patterns that consistently keep things on track.

## Start with Your Working Context

Instead of jumping straight to your request, establish the working relationship:

```
"I'm working on [specific project/goal]. I'll likely need to iterate 
on this a few times to get it right. Can you help me with [initial request], 
and let me know what additional context would be useful as we go?"
```

This signals you're planning multiple rounds and invites the AI to be a more active collaborator.

## The "Build Forward" Pattern

When the AI gives you something close but not quite right, don't start over. Build on it:

**Instead of:** "That's not what I wanted. Let me try again..."

**Try:** "This is helpful, especially [specific part]. Can you adjust [specific element] while keeping [what worked]?"

This maintains continuity and often gets you to the target faster than repeated fresh starts.

## Use Status Checks

Every 3-4 exchanges, pause and realign:

```
"Before we continue, are we still on the right track for [original goal]? 
Should I clarify anything about what I'm looking for?"
```

I learned this after too many conversations that gradually drifted into interesting but irrelevant territory.

## What Actually Breaks Down

**The AI forgets your constraints.** After 5-6 exchanges, it might suggest solutions that violate requirements you mentioned earlier. Keep a running note of your key constraints and re-state them periodically.

**You get solution fixation.** The AI might lock onto one approach when alternatives would work better. If you're stuck, try: "What if we approached this completely differently?"

**Scope creep happens fast.** One small addition leads to another until you're solving a different problem entirely. I now explicitly say "let's finish this piece first" when I catch scope expanding.

## Two Recovery Strategies

When conversations go sideways:

1. **The Reset**: "Let me step back and reframe this..." then restate your core need
2. **The Redirect**: "That's interesting, but let's get back to [specific focus]"

## Honest Limitations

This only works when the AI understands your domain reasonably well. For highly specialized or technical work, you'll spend more time explaining than collaborating. Also, some AIs lose thread context faster than others—learn your tool's memory limits and work within them.

The goal isn't to have human-like conversations. It's to establish a productive working rhythm that gets you better results with less frustration.
