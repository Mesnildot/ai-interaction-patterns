# Script for Structured AI-Assisted Decision Making

When you're juggling multiple AI conversations for complex decisions, things get messy fast. I've found that having a structured approach prevents you from losing track of key considerations or getting swayed by the last response you read.

## The Decision Framework Script

Here's the approach I use for any significant decision where AI input matters:

### 1. Decision Setup (5 minutes)
```
DECISION: [One clear sentence]
DEADLINE: [When you need to decide]
STAKES: [What happens if you get this wrong]
CONSTRAINTS: [Time, budget, resources, non-negotiables]
```

### 2. Multi-Angle AI Consultation
I always get at least three perspectives by framing the same question differently:

**Prompt A - Analytical:**
"Break down the decision between [X] and [Y]. What are the key factors I should weigh? What data would help me decide?"

**Prompt B - Risk-focused:**
"What could go wrong with each option? What are the second and third-order effects I might miss?"

**Prompt C - Implementation-focused:**
"If I chose [X], what would the first 30 days look like? What about [Y]?"

### 3. Capture and Compare


## What Actually Works

**The "Devil's Advocate" follow-up** is gold. After getting initial recommendations, I ask: "Argue against your top recommendation. What am I not seeing?" This catches blind spots consistently.

**Time-boxing conversations** prevents rabbit holes. I give myself 20 minutes max per AI session. When I go longer, diminishing returns kick in hard.

**The "sleep test"** - I draft my decision after AI consultation, then sit on it overnight. If I wake up second-guessing, I need more input or the decision isn't ready.

## What Doesn't Work

Don't ask AI to "make the decision for you." It'll give you an answer, but you won't own it psychologically. Instead, ask it to help you think through your own decision.

Avoid the "consensus trap" - getting multiple AI opinions doesn't mean averaging them. Sometimes the outlier perspective is most valuable.

## Three Approaches for Different Situations

**Quick decisions (< 1 hour):** Use just the analytical prompt + devil's advocate follow-up. Document your reasoning in 2-3 bullets.

**Medium complexity (same day):** Full three-prompt approach, but keep each conversation under 15 minutes.

**High stakes (multi-day):** Add a fourth angle - ask AI to help you identify what additional research or expert input would be most valuable.

The key insight: AI excels at helping you structure your thinking and catch blind spots, but the actual decision-making weight should stay with you. Use it as a thinking partner, not an oracle.
