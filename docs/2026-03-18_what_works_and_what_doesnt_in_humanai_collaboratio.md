# What Actually Works (and What Doesn't) in Daily Human-AI Collaboration

After hundreds of hours working alongside AI tools, I've learned that successful collaboration isn't about finding the "perfect prompt" - it's about building sustainable working patterns that account for both AI strengths and very real limitations.

## What Actually Works

**Treat AI as a thinking partner, not a magic button.** The most productive sessions happen when I approach AI like a capable colleague who needs context. Instead of asking "Write a marketing email," I'll say "I need to follow up with enterprise customers who attended our webinar but haven't scheduled demos. Here's what we covered..." This context-setting takes an extra 30 seconds but saves 10 minutes of back-and-forth refinement.

**Embrace the iterative dance.** My best work emerges through 3-4 rapid iterations rather than trying to nail everything in one shot. I'll start with a rough direction, let AI generate something, then build on what works while discarding what doesn't. This feels more natural than the old "specify everything upfront" approach I used to force.

**Use AI for grunt work, humans for judgment calls.** AI excels at first drafts, research synthesis, and format conversion. I excel at strategic decisions, emotional intelligence, and knowing what "feels right" for my audience. When I respect these boundaries, we both perform better.

## What Consistently Fails

**Outsourcing thinking entirely.** When I get lazy and ask AI to "figure out our Q4 strategy," the output is always generic corporate-speak. AI can help me think through strategy, but it can't replace the thinking itself.

**Expecting perfect accuracy on facts.** I've learned to verify anything involving specific data, dates, or technical specifications. AI will confidently state that Python 3.11 was released in 2019 (it wasn't) or misattribute quotes. Always cross-check factual claims.

**Using AI for sensitive communications.** Performance reviews, difficult customer situations, or personal conflicts need human judgment. AI often suggests overly diplomatic language that sounds robotic or misses important emotional nuances.

## Three Approaches That Work

1. **The iterative refinement approach**: Start broad, narrow down through conversation
2. **The expert interview approach**: Ask AI to role-play as a domain expert and interview them
3. **The structured prompt approach**: Use templates for recurring tasks (meeting summaries, project updates)

## The Honest Reality

Some days AI feels like a productivity superpower. Other days it's like working with an enthusiastic intern who needs constant supervision. The key is building workflows that work on both types of days - systems that enhance your thinking without becoming a crutch.

The goal isn't perfect AI collaboration; it's consistent, sustainable productivity that makes your work more interesting, not more dependent.
