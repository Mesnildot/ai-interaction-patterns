# Lessons from Daily AI Work: What I've Actually Learned

After months of integrating AI tools into my daily workflow, here's what actually matters—beyond the hype and horror stories.

## Start Small, Scale Thoughtfully

My biggest early mistake was trying to AI-fy everything at once. I threw Claude at brainstorming, ChatGPT at writing, GitHub Copilot at coding, and burned out managing all the different interfaces and contexts.

**What works better:** Pick one tool for one specific task you do repeatedly. Master that workflow first. I started with using Claude for initial draft reviews—just that. Once it became second nature (about 3 weeks), I expanded to other areas.

## Context is Your Currency

AI tools are context-hungry beasts. The difference between mediocre and genuinely useful output often comes down to how well you frame the problem.

**Effective approach:** I keep a "context template" for common tasks. For code reviews, I include: project background, coding standards, specific concerns, and examples of what good looks like. For writing, I specify audience, tone, constraints, and purpose.

**What doesn't work:** Lazy prompts like "make this better" or "fix my code." You get generic output that requires more work to customize than starting fresh.

## Build Your Quality Filters

AI output ranges from brilliant to confidently wrong. You need reliable ways to catch the latter.

**For code:** Always run tests, use linting, and do manual spot-checks on logic flow. Copilot suggestions often work syntactically but miss business logic nuances.

**For writing:** Read everything out loud. AI-generated text often has correct grammar but weird rhythm. Also, fact-check anything that sounds too convenient—AI confidently invents sources and statistics.

**For analysis:** Cross-reference conclusions with source data. AI tools sometimes cherry-pick patterns that confirm the narrative they've constructed.

## Manage the Feedback Loop

The most underrated skill is knowing when to iterate versus when to start over. I've wasted hours trying to prompt-engineer my way out of a fundamentally flawed AI response.

**Rule of thumb:** If you're on your fourth revision trying to fix the same issue, restart with a clearer initial prompt. Usually the problem is in your setup, not the AI's execution.

## The Human-AI Workflow Sweet Spot

AI excels at:
- First drafts and brainstorming
- Pattern recognition in large datasets
- Code boilerplate and common implementations
- Reformatting and restructuring existing content

You excel at:
- Strategic decisions and prioritization
- Understanding business context and constraints
- Quality judgment and creative leaps
- Handling edge cases and exceptions

The magic happens when you design workflows that leverage both strengths rather than treating AI as either a magic solution or a fancy autocomplete.

Most importantly: stay curious about what's not working. The tools are evolving rapidly, and your workflows should too.
