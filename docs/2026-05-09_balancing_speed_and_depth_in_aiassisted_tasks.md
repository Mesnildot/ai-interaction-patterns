# Balancing Speed and Depth in AI-Assisted Tasks

The constant tension between moving fast and going deep with AI tools is something I wrestle with daily. Here's what I've learned about finding the right balance.

## Start with the Speed Pass

For most tasks, I do a quick AI-assisted first pass to get the basic structure down. Whether it's writing, analysis, or problem-solving, this gives me a foundation to work from in minutes rather than hours. The key is treating this as scaffolding, not the final product.

**What works:** Ask for outlines, bullet points, or rough drafts first. Get the AI to map out the territory before diving into details.

**What doesn't:** Trying to get both speed AND depth in a single prompt. You'll get mediocre results that satisfy neither goal.

## Know When to Slow Down

Speed is great for exploration and initial drafts, but certain situations demand the deeper dive:

- **High-stakes decisions** where accuracy matters more than time
- **Creative work** that needs your unique perspective
- **Complex problems** with multiple variables or edge cases

I've learned to recognize the warning signs that I'm moving too fast: when AI responses feel generic, when I can't explain the reasoning behind suggestions, or when stakeholders start asking questions I can't answer.

## The 70-30 Rule

For most projects, I aim for about 70% of the value in the first 30% of the time using AI assistance. Then I decide: is this good enough, or does it need the remaining 70% of time for human refinement?

This isn't arbitrary—it's based on observing that AI excels at getting you to "pretty good" quickly, but the jump from "pretty good" to "excellent" usually requires significant human judgment and iteration.

## Three Practical Approaches

**The Iterative Method:** Start fast with AI, then cycle between human refinement and AI assistance. Good for writing and analysis where you can build quality progressively.

**The Parallel Track:** Use AI for rapid prototyping while simultaneously doing deeper manual work. Merge the best of both. I use this for research projects.

**The Validation Approach:** Let AI generate quickly, then use different methods (human review, alternative AI models, external sources) to verify and deepen the work.

## The Honest Reality

Sometimes you just need to pick a side. Tight deadlines mean accepting AI-assisted "good enough." Critical decisions mean slowing down regardless of pressure. 

The skill isn't in always finding perfect balance—it's in consciously choosing your tradeoff and being honest about what you're optimizing for. Speed when you need to move, depth when accuracy matters, and recognizing the difference between the two.
