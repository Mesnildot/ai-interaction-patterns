# Balancing Speed and Depth in AI-Assisted Tasks

Working with AI daily teaches you that the "go fast vs go deep" tension is real and constant. I've found three main approaches that work depending on what you're trying to accomplish.

## The Iterative Spiral

Start fast, then deepen selectively. When researching a new topic, I'll ask the AI for a broad overview first, then identify the 2-3 most critical areas that need depth. This beats trying to get everything perfect in one shot.

**Example**: Analyzing a market trend, I start with "Give me the key factors driving X trend" then follow up with "Now dive deep on regulatory impacts" for just the pieces that matter most to my decision.

**Works when**: You have time for 2-3 rounds and need both breadth and selective depth.
**Doesn't work when**: You're under extreme time pressure or dealing with highly technical domains where surface-level info is dangerous.

## The Speed-First Triage

Sometimes you genuinely need to move fast across multiple tasks. I use AI for rapid first-pass analysis, but I'm explicit about the limitations. I'll ask for "quick assessment" or "initial thoughts" and mark outputs as preliminary.

**What I've learned**: Speed-first works great for prioritization, brainstorming, and eliminating obviously bad options. It's terrible for final recommendations or anything involving safety/compliance.

## The Deep Dive Partnership

For complex problems, I treat the AI as a research partner rather than an answer machine. I share my thinking process, ask it to challenge my assumptions, and work through problems step-by-step. This is slower but catches nuances that rapid-fire questioning misses.

**Practical pattern**: "I'm thinking about X because of Y. What am I missing? What would make my reasoning stronger?"

## What Actually Matters

**Time pressure isn't the only factor** - task complexity and stakes matter more. I go fast on reversible decisions and brainstorming. I go deep on anything that affects other people or requires domain expertise I don't have.

**The AI doesn't know which mode you need**. You have to signal it explicitly. "Quick brainstorm" gets different output than "thorough analysis."

**Quality degrades predictably with speed**. Fast AI responses miss edge cases, oversimplify trade-offs, and tend toward generic advice. Sometimes that's fine. Sometimes it's not.

The key insight: Speed vs depth isn't a fixed choice - it's a dynamic balance you adjust based on stakes, reversibility, and how much you already know about the domain. Most of my best AI-assisted work uses speed for exploration and depth for decisions.
