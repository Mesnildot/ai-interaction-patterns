# Professional AI Tool Use: What Works (And What Doesn't)

After two years of integrating various AI tools into daily workflows, here's what actually matters when you're using these systems professionally.

## Start Small, Think Integration

The biggest mistake I see is trying to overhaul everything at once. I started with one specific task—drafting initial email responses—and learned the tool's quirks before expanding. This approach reveals where AI genuinely saves time versus where it creates more work.

**What works:** Pick one repetitive task that's low-stakes if the output needs tweaking. Email drafts, meeting summaries, or initial research are good starting points.

**What doesn't:** Jumping into high-stakes work like client presentations or financial analysis without understanding the tool's failure modes first.

## Develop Your "Garbage Detector"

AI tools produce confident-sounding nonsense regularly. After months of use, I've developed intuition for spotting problems:
- Generic language that could apply to any situation
- Statements that feel too convenient or neat
- Missing specific details I know should be there
- Answers that sidestep the actual question

The key is staying engaged enough to notice these patterns. Autopilot mode leads to publishing AI hallucinations.

## Iteration Beats Perfect Prompts

I used to spend 10 minutes crafting the "perfect" prompt. Now I start messy and iterate. A rough first attempt often reveals what the AI misunderstood, making the second prompt much more effective.

**Practical approach:** 
1. Quick, imperfect first prompt
2. Identify what's wrong with the output
3. Add one specific constraint or example
4. Repeat until useful

This usually takes less time than trying to anticipate every edge case upfront.

## Context Management Is Everything

AI tools forget context constantly, but they're also terrible at handling too much context. I've learned to be strategic:

- **For ongoing projects:** Maintain a "context document" with key details, decisions, and constraints that I paste into new conversations
- **For complex tasks:** Break them into smaller, focused sessions rather than one marathon conversation
- **For creative work:** Start fresh periodically—accumulated context can create tunnel vision

## The Human-AI Handoff

The most valuable skill I've developed is knowing when to take over. AI excels at generating options and handling routine elements, but human judgment is crucial for:
- Final quality assessment
- Understanding business context and politics
- Making judgment calls about tone and audience
- Connecting dots across different projects or time periods

I treat AI as a research assistant and first-draft generator, not a decision-maker. This keeps me engaged while capturing the efficiency benefits.

**Bottom line:** AI tools are powerful multipliers when you stay actively involved in the process. The sweet spot is letting them handle the mechanical work while you focus on strategy, quality control, and the uniquely human elements that matter most.
