# Balancing Speed and Depth in AI-assisted Tasks

After months of daily AI collaboration, I've learned that the biggest trap isn't using AI poorly—it's using it at the wrong speed for the task at hand.

## The Speed-Depth Spectrum

I think of AI assistance on a spectrum. Quick queries get me unstuck or generate starting points. Deep collaboration produces refined, nuanced work. The key is matching your approach to what you actually need.

**Quick hits** work great for:
- Brainstorming alternatives when you're stuck
- Getting unfamiliar syntax or formatting examples
- Rapid first drafts to see if an idea has legs
- Breaking through analysis paralysis

**Deep dives** pay off for:
- Complex reasoning that needs multiple iterations
- Creative work where tone and nuance matter
- Technical implementations with edge cases
- Anything you'll need to defend or explain to others

## Three Practical Approaches

**Time-box your initial attempt.** Give yourself 10 minutes with AI to see what you can get. If it's good enough, stop. If not, you've learned what the quick approach misses and can invest in deeper collaboration.

**Use the "explain back" test.** After getting AI output, try explaining it to someone else (or just out loud). If you stumble, you probably moved too fast and missed important understanding.

**Layer your iterations strategically.** Start broad, then narrow. First pass: "Help me understand X." Second pass: "Now help me solve the specific case where Y." Third pass: "Refine this solution for Z constraint." Each layer adds depth without starting over.

## What Actually Slows You Down

Counter-intuitively, the fastest approach often isn't the quickest queries. Vague prompts lead to generic responses that require more back-and-forth. I've wasted more time on "help me with this presentation" than on detailed setups that take 30 seconds longer to write.

**The hidden time sink:** Getting 80% solutions that take more work to fix than building properly from the start. If you're going to iterate anyway, be intentional about it.

## When to Pump the Brakes

Watch for these signals that you're moving too fast:
- AI responses feel generic or miss your context
- You're copying outputs without understanding them
- Solutions work but you can't modify them
- You're asking the same types of clarifying questions repeatedly

The goal isn't always maximum speed—it's matching your pace to the durability you need from the output.
